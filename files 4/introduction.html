Introduction

Understanding Sports Anxiety

What it is, what causes it, how it affects your performance — and why it's more common than you think.

What is Sports Anxiety?

Sports anxiety is the habit of viewing competitive situations — such as games or competitions — as threatening or frightful, causing apprehension and tension when engaging in the activity.

Common Causes

Anxiety in sports typically comes from two categories of stressors:

⚡ Performance Stressors

Stress related to how prepared you feel, thoughts about getting injured, and expectations you set for yourself about your own performance.

🤝 Organizational Stressors

Stress related to the competitive environment, your role on the team, and the expectations coaches and teammates have of you.

Common Symptoms

Sports anxiety can show up physically and mentally. Recognizing these is the first step:

  • Tremors or shakiness
  • Racing heart
  • Hyperventilation
  • Muscle tension
  • Bathroom troubles
  • Sweating
  • Self-doubt and negativity

How Does Sports Anxiety Impact Performance?

A little bit of anxiety before competitions can actually be helpful — it prepares you to meet the demands of the moment and can increase motivation. However, anxiety at elevated levels becomes harmful.

Higher levels of anxiety can cause decreased performance and increase the risk of injury, because stress increases muscle tension, narrows attention, and leads to coordination difficulties.

Breaking the Stigma

30–60% of athletes are affected by performance anxiety — you are not alone

According to the American College of Sports Medicine, 30% of women and 25% of men student-athletes report experiencing anxiety. Olympians and elite athletes — including Simone Biles — have spoken publicly about their own experiences with sports anxiety.

Experiencing anxiety before competition does not mean you are weak or underprepared. Anxiety can affect athletes at every level, and it is okay to seek help and support.

"I say put mental health first. Because if you don't then you're not going to enjoy your sport and you're not going to succeed as much as you want to."

— Simone Biles
