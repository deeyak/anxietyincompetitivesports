Mental Technique

Visualization

Mentally rehearse success before you step onto the field. Used by Olympians like Michael Phelps, visualization is one of the most powerful tools in sports psychology.

What is Visualization?

Visualization is mentally walking through a competition in order to reduce anxiety and improve performance. You create a mental script for how you want the competition to go — using your imagination to experience success before it happens.

Fun fact: Michael Phelps famously used visualization before every Olympic race.

How to Practice Visualization

Follow these steps to get the most out of your visualization session:

  1. Find a place free from distraction and get into a comfortable position, either sitting up or lying down.
  2. Close your eyes and take deep, slow breaths until you feel settled.
  3. Create a vivid, detailed scene — go into as much emotion and sensory detail as possible.
    • Picture past successes
    • Imagine the environment — what you're wearing, what you see, hear, and feel
    • See yourself performing well
    • Feel the emotions you want to carry into competition
    • If needed, imagine a relaxing place like the beach to ease into the exercise

Visualization Scripts by Sport

Select your sport below to read a guided visualization script you can follow before your next competition.

Soccer Visualization

Close your eyes and take a deep breath in through your nose and out through your mouth. Picture yourself stepping onto the soccer field. Feel the grass beneath your cleats and hear the familiar sounds around you — teammates talking, whistles blowing, the crowd in the distance. You feel prepared. Your body feels loose, strong, and ready.

As the game begins, imagine yourself moving confidently. You stay calm and focused. The ball comes to your feet and your first touch is controlled and smooth. You look up and clearly see the field around you. You make sharp passes, communicate with teammates, and trust your instincts. Your movements feel natural.

Picture yourself staying composed even during pressure moments. Instead of worrying about mistakes, you remain present. You recover quickly and refocus. Visualize making the play you want — a strong pass, defensive stop, assist, or goal. See yourself executing it successfully.

Notice how confident and relaxed you feel. Your breathing stays steady. You trust your preparation and training. You do not need to be perfect. You simply play your game.

Take one final deep breath and remind yourself: I am prepared, focused, and ready.

Football Visualization

Close your eyes and take a slow breath. Imagine arriving at the stadium. You hear pads clicking, teammates talking, and the crowd building with energy. Feel yourself becoming calm and focused.

Picture yourself stepping onto the field with confidence. Your body feels strong and ready. See yourself lining up before the snap. Instead of feeling pressure, you feel prepared.

Visualize your assignment becoming clear. If you are a receiver, imagine running crisp routes and making confident catches. If you are a quarterback, see yourself reading the field and delivering accurate throws. If you play defense, picture reacting quickly and making solid tackles.

Imagine pressure situations becoming opportunities. Your breathing remains controlled. You trust your training and react naturally. You execute your responsibilities with confidence.

See yourself making strong plays and helping your team. Feel proud of your effort and preparation.

Take a breath and remind yourself: I trust my preparation.

Basketball Visualization

Close your eyes and take a slow breath. Picture yourself walking into the gym. Hear sneakers squeaking on the court and basketballs bouncing around you. Feel calm and focused.

See yourself warming up. Your body feels loose and ready. Visualize your shot feeling smooth. Every movement feels controlled.

Picture the game beginning. See yourself handling the ball confidently. Your decisions feel natural. You see the court clearly. You make strong passes, communicate, move without hesitation, and stay present.

Imagine yourself shooting confidently. The ball leaves your hands with perfect form. Watch it drop through the net. Swish.

Even if mistakes happen, you reset immediately. You stay calm and focused on the next play.

Feel yourself enjoying the game and trusting your preparation. Repeat quietly: I play with confidence and trust myself.

Volleyball Visualization

Take a deep breath and picture yourself stepping onto the court. Hear teammates communicating and shoes moving across the floor.

Visualize yourself feeling loose, energized, and ready. See the serve crossing the net and your body reacting naturally.

Imagine making clean passes, strong sets, and confident swings. You move easily and communicate clearly with teammates.

Picture pressure situations. Instead of anxiety, you feel focus. You trust yourself and react naturally.

Visualize a strong play — an ace, a block, or a kill. Feel confident.

Repeat: I trust my training and stay present.

Track / Cross Country Visualization

Take a slow breath and picture yourself standing at the starting line. Feel the ground beneath your spikes. Hear the quiet anticipation around you.

Notice your breathing becoming calm. You feel prepared.

The race begins. Imagine yourself running smoothly and relaxed — picture the running form you want. Your stride feels efficient and powerful. You are focused only on your pace and movement.

Visualize yourself staying calm as fatigue appears. You continue breathing steadily and trust your training.

See yourself finishing strong. Feel pride as you cross the finish line.

Tell yourself: I am strong and ready.

Baseball / Softball Visualization

Take a slow breath and picture yourself walking onto the field. Feel the sun, the dirt beneath your cleats, and the glove in your hand.

Imagine stepping into the batter's box. You feel calm. You take a breath and settle in. The pitcher begins their motion. Everything slows down.

See the ball clearly. Your body reacts naturally. You stay relaxed and swing confidently. Visualize solid contact. Hear the crack of the bat and watch the ball travel exactly where you want it.

If you field, imagine reacting smoothly and confidently to the ball. Your movements feel automatic and controlled. If you pitch, imagine throwing the ball with speed and accuracy.

You trust your preparation and focus only on the current play.

Tell yourself: One pitch. One moment. Stay present.

Lacrosse Visualization

Close your eyes and picture yourself stepping onto the field. Feel the stick in your hands and the excitement around you.

Visualize yourself moving confidently. You catch passes cleanly, react quickly, and trust your instincts.

See yourself making smart decisions under pressure. Your breathing stays steady and your focus remains clear.

Imagine a successful moment — a goal, assist, defensive stop, or save.

Feel yourself playing freely and confidently.

Repeat: I trust myself.

Tennis Visualization

Take a deep breath and picture yourself walking onto the court. Hear the bounce of tennis balls and feel the racket in your hand.

Visualize yourself standing behind the baseline. You feel calm and focused.

Picture yourself serving confidently. See the toss and smooth motion. Watch the ball land exactly where you want.

Imagine staying composed between points. You take a breath, reset, and focus only on the next opportunity.

See yourself moving smoothly and striking confidently.

Tell yourself: One point at a time.

Swimming Visualization

Close your eyes and picture yourself standing behind the blocks. Hear the quiet sounds of the pool.

Take a breath and feel calm.

Visualize stepping onto the block. Your body feels powerful and prepared.

The signal sounds. You dive smoothly into the water and feel it surround you. Your stroke feels strong and effortless — imagine the form you want. You move with rhythm and confidence.

Feel yourself remaining calm throughout the race and finishing strong.

Repeat: Trust your rhythm.

Dance / Gymnastics Visualization

Close your eyes and picture yourself standing before your routine begins. Feel your breathing slow.

Imagine hearing the music begin or preparing for your first movement. Your body feels strong and ready.

Visualize yourself moving with confidence. Every motion feels smooth and natural.

If nerves appear, imagine them turning into energy and focus.

Picture yourself completing your routine successfully and feeling proud.

Take one final breath and repeat: I trust myself and my preparation.
