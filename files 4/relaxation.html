Relaxation

Calm Your Body,
Quiet Your Mind

Breathing exercises, grounding techniques, and progressive muscle relaxation to stop anxious thoughts in their tracks.

Relaxation techniques such as breathing exercises, grounding exercises, and progressive muscle relaxation can calm the mind and ground you to prevent the stream of anxious thoughts. Research shows that taking just 6 deep breaths within 30 seconds can significantly reduce blood pressure.

Tavoian, D., & Craighead, D. H. (2023). Deep breathing exercise at work. Frontiers in Physiology, 14, 1040091.

📦

Box Breathing

Follow this simple 4-step routine, repeating until you feel calm. Imagine tracing the sides of a box as you breathe.

  • 1
    Trace up the box — inhale for 4 seconds
  • 2
    Trace across the top — hold your breath for 4 seconds
  • 3
    Trace down the box — exhale for 4 seconds
  • 4
    Complete the box — hold the empty breath for 4 seconds

Repeat the cycle until you feel calm and grounded.

🌬️

4-7-8 Breathing

A calming breath pattern that activates your parasympathetic nervous system. Repeat until you feel at ease.

  • 1
    Inhale through your nose (mouth closed, tongue resting behind front teeth) for 4 seconds
  • 2
    Hold your breath for 7 seconds
  • 3
    Exhale through your mouth for 8 seconds, making a whooshing sound
🌿

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Exercise

Ease your state of mind by engaging your senses. This grounds you in the present moment and interrupts anxious thinking.

  • 5
    Name 5 things you can see
  • 4
    Name 4 things you can touch
  • 3
    Name 3 things you can hear
  • 2
    Name 2 things you can smell
  • 1
    Name 1 thing you can taste
💪

Progressive Muscle Relaxation (PMR)

PMR is a technique that reduces stress and physical tension by systematically tensing and then releasing muscle groups throughout the body.

Focus on different muscle groups: arms, legs, face, hands, core.

  • 1
    Tense one muscle group for 5 seconds while inhaling, then hold for 5 seconds
  • 2
    Suddenly release and relax that muscle for 10 seconds while exhaling
  • 3
    Repeat through different muscle groups, working from one end of the body to the other
